# Garden Layout Design - 40x40 Space

## Space Analysis

| Total Area | 1,600 sq ft |
|------------|-------------|
| People to feed | 7 (4 adults, 3 toddlers) |
| Recommended space | ~700 sq ft (100/person for fresh eating) |
| **Growing space** | **~700 sq ft** |

Maximized growing space with 2ft paths throughout. This provides:
- 14 vegetable beds (up from original 10)
- Room for perennials
- Dedicated flower bed
- Berry areas with netting

## Bed Specifications

### Main Vegetable Beds (14 beds)
- **Size:** 4ft x 8ft each = 32 sq ft per bed
- **Total:** 448 sq ft of intensive growing
- **Bed height:** 17" galvanized metal ([Vegega](https://www.vegega.com/) or [Vego Garden](https://www.vegogarden.com/))
- **Pit depth:** 18" excavated underneath each bed for hugelkultur logs
- **Total growing depth:** 35" (18" logs in pit + 17" soil in bed)
- **Path width:** 2ft between all beds and rows

### Why 4ft Wide?
- Reach center from either side without stepping in
- Standard lumber length (less cutting)
- Efficient use of materials

### Flower Bed
- **Size:** 4ft x 12ft = 48 sq ft
- Location near vegetables for pollinator access
- Consider: zinnias, cosmos, marigolds, sunflowers

### Perennial Strip
- **Size:** ~4ft x 24ft = 96 sq ft
- Asparagus (needs 3 years to establish)
- Rhubarb (2-3 plants is plenty)
- Horseradish (contained - spreads aggressively)
- Perennial herbs (oregano, thyme, rosemary, sage, tarragon, lavender)

### Berry Areas (2 sections)
- **Size:** ~6ft x 10ft each = 120 sq ft total
- Blueberries (need acidic soil amendment)
- Grapes (need trellis structure)
- Include PVC frame for bird netting on blueberries

## Path Design

### All Paths: 2ft wide
- Adequate walking access
- Wheelbarrow still fits (standard wheelbarrow is 24-30" wide)
- Mulch heavily with wood chips

### Total Path Area
Approximately 350-400 sq ft (reduced from 600 sq ft with 3ft paths)

## Water Access

**Status:** Hose faucet ~10ft from garden edge - ideal location.

### Back to Eden Watering Reality

**Good news:** BTE dramatically reduces watering needs.

| Stage | Watering Needed |
|-------|-----------------|
| Year 1 (establishing) | Moderate - water transplants, new seeds |
| Year 2 | Reduced - mostly during dry spells |
| Year 3+ | Minimal - some gardeners water only a few times per YEAR |

The thick wood chip mulch:
- Holds moisture at root level
- Reduces evaporation by 60-80%
- Mimics forest floor conditions

### Recommendation: Simple Setup

Given BTE's low water needs, you may not need full drip irrigation:

**Option A: Soaker hose under mulch (~$50-75)**
- Run soaker hose through beds
- Cover with mulch
- Turn on manually during dry spells
- Simplest, cheapest, probably sufficient

**Option B: Drip with timer (~$150-300)**
- Set and forget
- Better for Year 1 while establishing
- May become unnecessary by Year 3

**For your time constraints:** Start with drip + timer for Year 1, evaluate if you even need it after that. Many BTE gardeners stop watering almost entirely after establishment.

Sources:
- [Epic Gardening - Back to Eden](https://www.epicgardening.com/back-to-eden-gardening/)
- [Melissa K. Norris - Wood Chips for Mulch](https://melissaknorris.com/wood-chips-for-garden-mulch-beneficial-or-not/)

## Materials Estimate (Raised Beds)

### RECOMMENDATION: 17" Galvanized Metal Beds + Pit Excavation

**Why this approach wins:**
- 20-30 year lifespan (longer than cedar)
- No rot, no termites, no maintenance
- Pits allow for large (18" diameter) hugelkultur logs
- Cheaper than 32" tall beds even with excavation cost
- Better hugelkultur performance (logs insulated by earth)

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|

| Volume per pit | ~1.8 cubic yards |
| Total excavation | ~25 cubic yards (14 beds) |
| Equipment | Mini excavator (few hours) |
| Labor cost | $300-600 total |

### Fill Material Per Bed

For hugelkultur/BTE with pit excavation:
| Layer | Location | Material | Depth |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Bottom** | In pit | Logs (18" diameter) | 18" |
| **Middle** | In bed | Leaves/branches/compost | 6-8" |
| **Top** | In bed | Mushroom compost + soil | 8-10" |

**Mushroom compost tip:** Buy in bulk (cubic yard) - much cheaper than bags. One cubic yard fills ~3-4 beds (top layer). **TEST pH BEFORE BUYING** - see [soil-ph-composition.md](soil-ph-composition.md).

## Phased Build Option

If budget or time is tight, build in phases:

**Year 1:**
- Deer fence (critical)
- 4-6 raised beds
- Focus on easy wins: tomatoes, squash, greens, beans

**Year 2:**
- Add remaining beds
- Plant perennials (asparagus, berries)
- Add bird netting structure

**Year 3:**
- Full production
- Fine-tune what works for your family
