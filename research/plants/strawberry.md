# Strawberries

## Overview
Strawberries are a rewarding perennial fruit. Home-grown berries are far more flavorful than store-bought. Plants produce for 3-5 years.

## Varieties
- **June-bearing** - Large harvest in early summer
- **Everbearing** - Smaller harvests spring through fall
- **Day-neutral** - Continuous smaller harvests

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 5.5-6.8 |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | Year 2 for best production |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: Early spring or fall
- **Harvest**: June (June-bearing) or all season (everbearing)
- **Remove Flowers Year 1**: For stronger plants

## Care Tips
1. **Remove Year 1 Flowers** - Builds stronger plants
2. **Mulch with Straw** - Keeps berries clean, retains moisture
3. **Control Runners** - Or let them fill in bed
4. **Renovate After Harvest** - Mow and thin June-bearers

## Common Issues
- **Birds** - Netting essential
- **Slugs** - Straw mulch, beer traps
- **Gray Mold** - Pick promptly, good air flow
- **Verticillium Wilt** - Avoid planting after tomatoes/peppers

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beans, lettuce, spinach, thyme
Avoid planting near: Cabbage family, fennel

## Expected Yield
1-2 pints per plant per year
