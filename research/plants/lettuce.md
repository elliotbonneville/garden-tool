# Lettuce

## Overview
Lettuce is a cool-season crop perfect for spring and fall. Leaf lettuce is cut-and-come-again, providing multiple harvests.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Partial shade to full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 6-12 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 30-60 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Sow**: April 1 - May 15
- **Fall Sow**: August 1 - September 15
- **Summer**: Provide shade or skip (bolts in heat)

## Care Tips
1. **Succession Plant** - Sow every 2 weeks
2. **Shade in Summer** - Use shade cloth or taller plants
3. **Cut-and-Come-Again** - Harvest outer leaves for continuous production
4. **Cool Soil** - Mulch heavily

## Common Issues
- **Bolting** - Heat causes plants to go to seed
- **Slugs** - Beer traps, diatomaceous earth
- **Tip Burn** - Calcium deficiency or heat stress

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Carrots, radishes, strawberries, chives
Avoid planting near: None significant

## Expected Yield
1/2 - 1 lb per plant
