# Tomatillo

## Overview
Tomatillos are essential for authentic salsa verde. These prolific plants are easier than tomatoes and produce abundantly. Plant at least two for pollination.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Toma Verde** - Classic green, reliable
- **Purple** - Sweeter, purple color
- **Grande Rio Verde** - Large fruits

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 24-36 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 75-100 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: April 1-15
- **Transplant**: May 20-June 5
- **Harvest**: August-frost

## Care Tips
1. **Plant at least 2** - Cross-pollination required
2. **Cage or stake** - Plants get large and sprawling
3. **Harvest when husks split** - Fruit fills the papery husk
4. **Let some drop** - Self-seeds vigorously

## Common Issues
- **No fruit** - Need 2+ plants for pollination
- **Sprawling growth** - Normal, support helps
- **Volunteer seedlings** - Can become weedy

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Peppers, basil, parsley
Avoid planting near: Fennel, brassicas

## Expected Yield
- 2-3 lbs per plant
