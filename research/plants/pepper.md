# Peppers

## Overview
Peppers are warm-season crops that produce colorful, flavorful fruits. Both sweet and hot varieties grow well in Zone 6b with adequate heat.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Bell Peppers** - Sweet, thick-walled, great for cooking
- **Hot Peppers** - Adds spice to dishes, prolific producers

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (8+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Spacing | 18-24 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 60-90 days |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: February 15 - March 15
- **Transplant Outdoors**: May 20-30 (soil temp 65Â°F+)
- **First Harvest**: July - August

## Care Tips
1. **Warmth** - Peppers love heat; use black plastic mulch to warm soil
2. **Staking** - Support heavy-laden plants
3. **Patience** - Let peppers fully color for best flavor
4. **Consistent Water** - Prevents blossom drop

## Common Issues
- **Blossom Drop** - Caused by temperature extremes or stress
- **Aphids** - Spray with water or use insecticidal soap
- **Sunscald** - Provide afternoon shade in extreme heat

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, basil, carrots, onions
Avoid planting near: Fennel, kohlrabi

## Expected Yield
- Bell: 5-10 peppers per plant
- Hot: 20-50+ peppers per plant
