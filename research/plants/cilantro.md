# Cilantro

## Overview
Cilantro is a fast-growing herb that bolts quickly in heat. Succession plant for continuous harvest. The seeds (coriander) are also edible.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Santo** - Slow-bolting variety
- **Calypso** - Very slow to bolt
- **Leisure** - Good leaf production

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.2-6.8 |
| Spacing | 6-8 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 45-70 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Sowing**: April 1-May 15
- **Fall Sowing**: August 15-September 15
- **Succession Plant**: Every 2-3 weeks
- **Harvest**: Ongoing until bolt

## Care Tips
1. **Direct sow** - Doesn't transplant well
2. **Part shade in summer** - Delays bolting
3. **Let some bolt** - Self-seeds for next season
4. **Harvest coriander** - Collect seeds when brown

## Common Issues
- **Bolting** - Plant in cool weather, succession sow
- **Aphids** - Spray with water, use insecticidal soap
- **Powdery mildew** - Ensure air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, peppers, spinach, legumes
Avoid planting near: Fennel

## Expected Yield
- Multiple harvests per plant before bolting
