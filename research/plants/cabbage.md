# Cabbage

## Overview
Cabbage is a cool-season crop that produces dense, nutritious heads. It's relatively easy to grow in Zone 6b with proper pest management.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Early Jersey Wakefield** - Pointed heads, early
- **Golden Acre** - Round heads, compact
- **Red Express** - Beautiful purple-red color

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 70-100 days |
| Water | 1-1.5 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Crop Start Indoors**: March 1-15
- **Transplant**: April 15-May 1
- **Fall Crop Direct Sow**: July 1-15
- **Harvest**: June-July (spring), Oct-Nov (fall)

## Care Tips
1. **Consistent moisture** - Prevents cracking
2. **Row covers** - Protect from cabbage moths
3. **Firm soil** - Helps heads form properly
4. **Harvest when firm** - Don't wait too long

## Common Issues
- **Cabbage worms** - Use BT spray or row covers
- **Splitting** - Caused by uneven watering
- **Clubroot** - Rotate crops, maintain pH above 7

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Celery, dill, onions, potatoes
Avoid planting near: Strawberries, tomatoes

## Expected Yield
- 1 head per plant (2-5 lbs each)
