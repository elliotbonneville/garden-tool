# Arugula

## Overview
Arugula is a fast-growing, peppery green that adds flavor to salads. Cool-season crop that bolts quickly in heat.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Partial shade to full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 30-40 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Sow**: March 15 - May 1
- **Fall Sow**: August 15 - September 30
- **Summer**: Too hot, will bolt immediately

## Care Tips
1. **Fast Growing** - Ready in about a month
2. **Succession Plant** - Every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
3. **Harvest Young** - Before flowering for mildest flavor
4. **Let Some Bolt** - Flowers are edible and attract pollinators

## Common Issues
- **Flea Beetles** - Row cover, plant near radishes as trap crop
- **Bolting** - Plant in cool weather only
- **Bitterness** - More intense when stressed or old

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Bush beans, beets, carrots, lettuce
Avoid planting near: Strawberries

## Expected Yield
Multiple harvests per planting
