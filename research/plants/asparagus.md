# Asparagus

## Overview
Asparagus is a perennial vegetable that produces for 15-20+ years once established. Requires patience but rewards with reliable spring harvests.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.5-7.5 |
| Spacing | 18 inches apart, rows 4-5 feet apart |
| Days to Harvest | 2-3 years to first harvest |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant Crowns**: April (as soon as soil can be worked)
- **Harvest**: April - June (after 2-3 year establishment)
- **Stop Harvesting**: Mid-June (let ferns grow)

## Care Tips
1. **Be Patient** - Don't harvest first 2 years
2. **Let Ferns Grow** - They feed next year's spears
3. **Cut Ferns in Fall** - After they yellow
4. **Mulch Heavily** - Suppress weeds, retain moisture

## Common Issues
- **Thin Spears** - Over-harvesting, needs more establishment time
- **Asparagus Beetles** - Hand pick, neem oil
- **Crown Rot** - Ensure good drainage

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, parsley, basil
Avoid planting near: Garlic, onions, potatoes

## Expected Yield
0.5 lb per plant per year (mature plants)
