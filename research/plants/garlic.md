# Garlic

## Overview
Garlic is one of the easiest crops to grow. Plant in fall, forget about it, and harvest large bulbs the following summer.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Hardneck** - Better for cold climates, produces scapes
- **Softneck** - Longer storage, milder flavor

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 8-9 months (fall to summer) |
| Water | 1 inch per week (stop 2 weeks before harvest) |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Fall Plant**: October 15 - November 15
- **Scape Harvest**: June (hardneck only)
- **Bulb Harvest**: July (when lower leaves brown)

## Care Tips
1. **Plant Pointy End Up** - 2 inches deep
2. **Mulch Heavily** - 4-6 inches of straw after planting
3. **Harvest Scapes** - Curly green shoots are delicious
4. **Cure Properly** - Hang in dry, airy spot for 2-4 weeks

## Common Issues
- **Small Bulbs** - Plant larger cloves, adequate spacing
- **Rot** - Ensure good drainage, don't overwater
- **Rust** - Remove infected leaves, improve air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, peppers, carrots, fruit trees
Avoid planting near: Beans, peas

## Expected Yield
1 bulb per clove planted
