# Kale

## Overview
Kale is an extremely hardy, nutritious green that can be harvested all season. Flavor improves after frost. Perfect for cut-and-come-again harvesting.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Lacinato (Dinosaur) Kale** - Dark, bumpy leaves, mild flavor

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to partial shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 55-75 days |
| Water | 1-1.5 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Transplant**: April 15 - May 15
- **Fall Direct Sow**: July 15 - August 15
- **Overwinter**: Can survive mild winters with protection

## Care Tips
1. **Cut-and-Come-Again** - Harvest outer leaves, center keeps producing
2. **Cold Hardy** - Frost sweetens the flavor
3. **Steady Water** - Prevents tough, bitter leaves
4. **Don't Pull** - Keep harvesting single leaves all season

## Common Issues
- **Cabbage Worms** - BT spray, row cover
- **Aphids** - Strong spray of water, insecticidal soap
- **Flea Beetles** - Row cover early in season

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beets, celery, onions, potatoes
Avoid planting near: Strawberries, tomatoes

## Expected Yield
1-2 lbs per plant over the season
