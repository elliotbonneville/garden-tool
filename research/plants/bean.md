# Bush Beans

## Overview
Bush beans are easy-to-grow, high-yielding vegetables that also fix nitrogen in the soil. Great for succession planting.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Green Bush Beans** - Classic snap beans
- **Yellow Bush Beans** - Wax beans, milder flavor

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 50-60 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: May 15 - July 15 (succession plant every 2-3 weeks)
- **First Harvest**: July - October

## Care Tips
1. **Succession Plant** - Sow every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
2. **Don't Transplant** - Direct sow only, beans don't like root disturbance
3. **Harvest Young** - Before seeds bulge for best texture
4. **Don't Work Wet** - Avoid touching plants when wet (spreads disease)

## Common Issues
- **Mexican Bean Beetles** - Hand-pick, row cover
- **Rust** - Improve air circulation, avoid overhead watering
- **Poor Germination** - Soil too cold or wet

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Carrots, cucumbers, corn, potatoes
Avoid planting near: Onions, garlic

## Expected Yield
1/2 lb per foot of row
