# Cucumbers

## Overview
Cucumbers are refreshing summer vegetables perfect for salads, snacking, and pickling. Bush varieties work well in raised beds.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Bush Cucumbers** - Compact plants, no trellis needed
- **Pickling Cucumbers** - Smaller fruits for preserving

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 12-24 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 50-70 days |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: May 20 - June 10
- **First Harvest**: July - August

## Care Tips
1. **Consistent Water** - Bitter fruits from drought stress
2. **Harvest Often** - Encourages more production
3. **Morning Harvest** - Fruits are crispest
4. **Mulch Well** - Keeps roots cool and moist

## Common Issues
- **Cucumber Beetles** - Row cover early, trap crops
- **Powdery Mildew** - Choose resistant varieties
- **Bitter Fruits** - Usually from inconsistent watering

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beans, corn, peas, radishes, sunflowers
Avoid planting near: Potatoes, aromatic herbs

## Expected Yield
10-20 cucumbers per plant
