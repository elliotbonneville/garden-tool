# Rosemary

## Overview
Rosemary is a woody perennial herb with intense flavor. It's borderline hardy in Zone 6b - choose cold-hardy varieties or grow in containers to overwinter indoors.

- **Arp** - Most cold-hardy variety (to -10Â°F)
- **Hill Hardy** - Good cold tolerance
- **Salem** - Upright, cold tolerant

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 24-36 inches apart |
| Years to Maturity | 2-3 years |
| Water | Low - drought tolerant |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: Spring after frost
- **Harvest**: Year-round once established
- **Winter Protection**: Mulch heavily or bring indoors

## Care Tips
1. **Excellent drainage** - Critical for survival
2. **Don't overwater** - Prefers dry conditions
3. **Winter protection** - Mulch crown, or grow in pot
4. **Prune after flowering** - Maintains shape

## Common Issues
- **Winter kill** - Use hardy varieties, protect roots
- **Root rot** - Improve drainage
- **Powdery mildew** - Ensure air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Sage, thyme, lavender, beans, cabbage
Avoid planting near: Cucumbers, squash

## Expected Yield
- Year-round harvesting of sprigs
