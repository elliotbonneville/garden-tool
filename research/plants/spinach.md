# Spinach

## Overview
Spinach is a nutrient-dense cool-season green that grows quickly in spring and fall. Bolts in heat, so timing is important.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Partial shade to full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.5-7.5 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 40-50 days |
| Water | 1-1.5 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Sow**: March 15 - April 30
- **Fall Sow**: August 15 - September 30
- **Avoid**: Late spring through summer (bolts)

## Care Tips
1. **Early Start** - Can handle light frost
2. **Shade Cloth** - Extends season in warm weather
3. **Harvest Outer Leaves** - For continuous production
4. **Rich Soil** - Heavy feeder, benefits from compost

## Common Issues
- **Bolting** - Plant heat-tolerant varieties, provide shade
- **Leaf Miners** - Row cover prevention
- **Downy Mildew** - Good air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Strawberries, peas, beans, brassicas
Avoid planting near: None significant

## Expected Yield
1/4 - 1/2 lb per plant
