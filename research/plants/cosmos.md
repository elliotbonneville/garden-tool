# Cosmos

## Overview
Cosmos are delicate, daisy-like flowers that thrive on neglect. Perfect for filling gaps and attracting pollinators with minimal effort.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Days to Bloom | 60-90 days from seed |
| Water | Drought tolerant |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: After last frost (mid-May)
- **Bloom Period**: July through frost
- **Note**: Self-seeds readily

## Care Tips
1. **Poor Soil is Fine** - Rich soil = all leaves, few flowers
2. **Don't Fertilize** - Thrives on neglect
3. **May Need Staking** - Tall varieties
4. **Deadhead or Let Self-Seed** - Your choice

## Common Issues
- **Floppy Stems** - Stake tall varieties, or plant shorter types
- **Few Flowers** - Too much nitrogen, stop fertilizing
- **Virtually Problem-Free** - Very easy to grow

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, squash (attracts pollinators)
Use as: Pollinator plant, cut flower, garden filler

## Garden Benefits
- Excellent pollinator plant
- Self-seeds for next year
- Tolerates poor soil
- Long bloom season
