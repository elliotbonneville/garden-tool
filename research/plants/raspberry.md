# Raspberries

## Overview
Raspberries are a delicious perennial fruit that produce for many years. Choose between summer-bearing and fall-bearing (everbearing) types.

## Varieties
- **Summer-bearing** - One large harvest in early summer, grows on 2-year-old canes
- **Fall-bearing (Everbearing)** - Harvest late summer through frost, simpler pruning

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 5.5-6.5 |
| Spacing | 2-3 feet apart, rows 6-8 feet apart |
| Days to Harvest | Year 2+ for full production |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: Early spring (bare root) or fall
- **Summer Harvest**: June-July (summer-bearing)
- **Fall Harvest**: August-October (fall-bearing)

## Care Tips
1. **Provide Support** - Trellis or fence
2. **Prune Correctly** - Depends on variety type
3. **Mulch Heavily** - Shallow roots need protection
4. **Control Spread** - Can become invasive

## Common Issues
- **Japanese Beetles** - Hand pick, traps
- **Cane Borers** - Prune out affected canes
- **Raspberry Crown Borer** - Remove and destroy affected plants
- **Viruses** - Buy certified disease-free plants

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Garlic, tansy, yarrow
Avoid planting near: Blackberries (disease spread), nightshades

## Expected Yield
1-2 quarts per plant per year (mature plants)
