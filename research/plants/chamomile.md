# Chamomile

## Overview
Chamomile produces cheerful daisy-like flowers used for calming tea. German chamomile is annual; Roman is perennial. Both are easy and self-seed.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **German Chamomile** - Annual, more flowers, tea variety
- **Roman Chamomile** - Perennial, ground cover, apple scent

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 5.6-7.5 |
| Spacing | 6-8 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 60-90 days |
| Water | Moderate, drought tolerant |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: Early spring or fall
- **Bloom Time**: June-August
- **Harvest Flowers**: When fully open, ongoing

## Care Tips
1. **Surface sow** - Seeds need light to germinate
2. **Let self-seed** - Returns yearly
3. **Harvest frequently** - Encourages more blooms
4. **Dry flowers** - For tea storage

## Common Issues
- **Aphids** - Attract beneficial insects, spray off
- **Powdery mildew** - Good air circulation
- **Floppy growth** - Normal, don't overfertilize

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Cabbage, onions, cucumbers (improves flavor)
Avoid planting near: Mint (competes)

## Expected Yield
- Continuous flower harvest through summer
