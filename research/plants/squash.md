# Squash

## Overview
Squash includes summer varieties (zucchini, yellow) and winter types (butternut, spaghetti). Summer squash produces quickly; winter squash stores for months.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Butternut** - Sweet, stores well, compact vines
- **Spaghetti Squash** - Unique texture, stores well
- **Yellow Squash** - Summer type, prolific producer

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Spacing | 24-36 inches (bush), 4-6 feet (vining) |
| Days to Harvest | 50-60 (summer), 80-110 (winter) |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: April 15-May 1
- **Direct Sow/Transplant**: May 20-June 10
- **Summer Squash Harvest**: July-frost
- **Winter Squash Harvest**: September-October

## Care Tips
1. **Hill planting** - Improves drainage
2. **Hand pollinate** - If poor fruit set
3. **Harvest summer squash small** - 6-8 inches
4. **Cure winter squash** - 10 days in sun before storage

## Common Issues
- **Squash vine borer** - Check stems, remove borers
- **Powdery mildew** - Inevitable late season, plant early
- **Poor pollination** - Hand pollinate morning flowers

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Corn, beans (Three Sisters), radishes
Avoid planting near: Potatoes

## Expected Yield
- Summer: 6-10 lbs per plant
- Winter: 3-5 fruits per plant
