# Grapes

## Overview
Grapes are vigorous vining plants that produce fruit for decades. They require sturdy support and annual pruning but reward with abundant harvests.

## Varieties for Zone 6b
- **Concord** - Classic American grape, very hardy
- **Niagara** - White/green, good fresh eating
- **Mars** - Seedless, blue, cold-hardy
- **Reliance** - Seedless, red, very hardy

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (8+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 5.5-6.5 |
| Spacing | 6-8 feet apart |
| Years to First Harvest | 3 years |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: Early spring
- **First Fruit**: Year 3
- **Full Production**: Year 5+
- **Harvest**: September-October

## Care Tips
1. **Strong trellis** - Essential, plan before planting
2. **Prune heavily in late winter** - While dormant
3. **Train to trellis** - One or two main trunks
4. **Bird netting** - Protect ripening fruit

## Common Issues
- **Japanese beetles** - Hand pick, traps
- **Black rot** - Remove infected fruit, improve air flow
- **Birds** - Net before fruit ripens
- **Overgrowth** - Prune aggressively

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Hyssop, clover, geraniums
Avoid planting near: Cabbage, radishes

## Expected Yield
- 10-20+ lbs per mature vine
