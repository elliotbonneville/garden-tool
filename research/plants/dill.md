# Dill

## Overview
Dill is a versatile herb used fresh, dried, or for pickling. It attracts beneficial insects and self-seeds readily.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Bouquet** - Standard variety, good for seeds
- **Fernleaf** - Compact, slow to bolt
- **Mammoth** - Tall, excellent for pickles

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 5.5-6.5 |
| Spacing | 12 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 40-60 days (leaves), 85-115 days (seeds) |
| Water | Moderate, don't overwater |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: April 15 - July 1
- **Succession Plant**: Every 3 weeks
- **Leaf Harvest**: Ongoing
- **Seed Harvest**: Late summer

## Care Tips
1. **Direct sow only** - Taproot doesn't transplant
2. **Stake tall varieties** - Prevents wind damage
3. **Let it flower** - Attracts beneficials, produces seeds
4. **Self-seeds** - Allow some to go to seed

## Common Issues
- **Aphids** - Attract ladybugs, spray with water
- **Caterpillars** - Swallowtail larvae - consider leaving them
- **Bolting** - Plant successively

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Cabbage, cucumbers, lettuce, onions
Avoid planting near: Carrots, tomatoes

## Expected Yield
- Continuous leaf harvest plus seed heads
