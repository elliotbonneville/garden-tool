# Ground Cherries

## Overview
Ground cherries are tomato relatives producing sweet, golden berries in papery husks. Unique tropical flavor - like pineapple meets tomato.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Aunt Molly's** - Classic heirloom, very sweet
- **Cossack Pineapple** - Extra sweet, pineapple notes
- **Goldie** - Large fruits, productive

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Spacing | 24-36 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 70-80 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: March 15-April 1
- **Transplant**: May 20-June 5
- **Harvest**: August until frost

## Care Tips
1. **Start early indoors** - Long season like tomatoes
2. **Let fruits drop** - Ripe when they fall off
3. **Sprawling habit** - Can cage or let sprawl
4. **Watch for volunteers** - Self-seeds prolifically

## Common Issues
- **Same pests as tomatoes** - Hornworms, flea beetles
- **Overabundance** - Plants are very productive
- **Volunteers everywhere** - Can become weedy

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Basil, parsley, carrots
Avoid planting near: Fennel, brassicas

## Expected Yield
- Hundreds of fruits per plant
