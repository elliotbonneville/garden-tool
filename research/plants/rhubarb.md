# Rhubarb

## Overview
Rhubarb is a hardy perennial that produces tart stalks perfect for pies and jams. Once established, it's virtually indestructible and produces for decades.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to partial shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Spacing | 3-4 feet apart |
| Days to Harvest | Year 2+ |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant Crowns**: Early spring or fall
- **Harvest**: April - June (year 2 onward)
- **Stop Harvesting**: After 8-10 weeks

## Care Tips
1. **Never Eat Leaves** - Toxic (oxalic acid)
2. **Don't Harvest Year 1** - Let plant establish
3. **Pull, Don't Cut** - Twist and pull stalks
4. **Remove Flower Stalks** - Redirects energy to leaves

## Common Issues
- **Thin Stalks** - Needs division, or over-harvested
- **Crown Rot** - Improve drainage
- **Slow to Establish** - Normal, be patient

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Garlic, onions, cabbage family
Avoid planting near: Dock, other rhubarb (competition)

## Expected Yield
2-3 lbs per plant per year (mature plants)
