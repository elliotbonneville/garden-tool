# Sunflowers

## Overview
Sunflowers are cheerful, easy-to-grow annuals that provide food for pollinators and birds. Range from 2-foot dwarfs to 12-foot giants.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 6-36 inches (depending on variety) |
| Days to Bloom | 70-100 days |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: After last frost (mid-May)
- **Succession Plant**: Every 2 weeks for continuous bloom
- **Bloom Period**: July through September

## Care Tips
1. **Direct Sow** - Deep taproots don't transplant well
2. **Stake Tall Varieties** - Support heavy heads
3. **Succession Plant** - For extended bloom
4. **Leave Seed Heads** - Feed birds in fall

## Common Issues
- **Squirrels/Birds** - Cover developing heads if saving seeds
- **Lodging** - Stake, plant against fence
- **Powdery Mildew** - Good air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Corn, squash, cucumbers
Use as: Trap crop for aphids, windbreak, bird food

## Garden Benefits
- Attracts bees and butterflies
- Seeds feed birds
- Kids love growing them
- Natural garden focal point
