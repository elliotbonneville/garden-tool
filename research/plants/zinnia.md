# Zinnias

## Overview
Zinnias are vibrant, easy-to-grow annual flowers that bloom prolifically all summer. Excellent for cutting gardens and attract pollinators.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 5.5-7.5 |
| Spacing | 4-24 inches (depending on variety) |
| Days to Bloom | 60-70 days from seed |
| Water | 1 inch per week at base |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: After last frost (mid-May)
- **Bloom Period**: July through frost
- **Note**: Don't start indoors - dislikes transplanting

## Care Tips
1. **Direct Sow** - Grows fast, dislikes root disturbance
2. **Cut Flowers Often** - Promotes more blooms
3. **Water at Base** - Avoid wetting foliage
4. **Deadhead** - Or cut for bouquets

## Common Issues
- **Powdery Mildew** - Water at soil level, good spacing
- **Leaf Spot** - Remove affected leaves
- **Japanese Beetles** - Hand pick, trap crops

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Any vegetable garden (attracts pollinators)
Use as: Cut flower, pollinator plant, trap crop

## Garden Benefits
- Attracts butterflies and bees
- Long-lasting cut flowers
- Easy for beginners
