# Parsnips

## Overview
Parsnips are sweet root vegetables that improve dramatically after frost. They require patience - long growing season but worth the wait.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Hollow Crown** - Classic heirloom
- **Harris Model** - Smooth, uniform roots
- **Gladiator** - Fast maturing, canker resistant

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Spacing | 3-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 100-130 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: April 1-May 15
- **Harvest**: After frost, October-March
- **Can overwinter**: Mulch heavily, dig as needed

## Care Tips
1. **Fresh seed only** - Viability drops quickly
2. **Direct sow** - Taproot doesn't transplant
3. **Be patient** - Slow to germinate (2-3 weeks)
4. **Frost required** - Converts starch to sugar

## Common Issues
- **Poor germination** - Use fresh seed, keep moist
- **Forked roots** - Caused by rocks or fresh manure
- **Canker** - Choose resistant varieties

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Radishes (mark row), onions, peas
Avoid planting near: Carrots, celery (same pests)

## Expected Yield
- 3-5 lbs per 10-foot row
