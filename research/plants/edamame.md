# Edamame

## Overview
Edamame are immature soybeans harvested green. They're protein-rich, easy to grow, and fix nitrogen in the soil.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Midori Giant** - Large beans, early
- **Butterbean** - Sweet, reliable
- **Chiba Green** - Classic flavor, good yields

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 75-90 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week, more when flowering |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow**: May 15-June 15 (soil 60Â°F+)
- **Succession Plant**: Every 2 weeks until July 1
- **Harvest**: August-September

## Care Tips
1. **Use inoculant** - Improves nitrogen fixation
2. **Direct sow only** - Doesn't transplant well
3. **Harvest when pods plump** - Before yellowing
4. **Pull whole plant** - Easiest harvest method

## Common Issues
- **Rabbits** - Love young plants, use fencing
- **Mexican bean beetles** - Hand pick, neem oil
- **Poor germination** - Plant in warm soil

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Corn, squash, potatoes
Avoid planting near: Onions, garlic

## Expected Yield
- 1/2 to 1 lb per plant
