# Shallots

## Overview
Shallots are gourmet alliums with a mild, complex flavor. They multiply from single bulbs planted in fall or early spring.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **French Red** - Classic culinary shallot
- **Dutch Yellow** - Milder, good storage
- **Ambition** - Large bulbs, easy to grow

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 6-8 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 90-120 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Fall Planting**: October-November (preferred)
- **Spring Planting**: March-April
- **Harvest**: July-August

## Care Tips
1. **Plant like garlic** - Individual cloves, pointed end up
2. **Fall planting produces larger bulbs** - Overwinters well
3. **Stop watering when tops fall** - 2 weeks before harvest
4. **Cure before storage** - Dry for 2 weeks

## Common Issues
- **Bolting** - More common with spring planting
- **Small bulbs** - Needs full sun, good soil
- **Rot in storage** - Cure thoroughly, store cool and dry

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beets, carrots, lettuce
Avoid planting near: Beans, peas

## Expected Yield
- 5-10 bulbs per planted bulb
