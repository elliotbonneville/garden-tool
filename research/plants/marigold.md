# Marigolds

## Overview
Marigolds are classic companion plants believed to repel pests. Easy to grow, drought tolerant, and bloom continuously until frost.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 8-18 inches (depending on variety) |
| Days to Bloom | 50-60 days from seed |
| Water | Drought tolerant once established |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: 4-6 weeks before last frost
- **Transplant/Direct Sow**: After last frost (mid-May)
- **Bloom Period**: June through frost

## Care Tips
1. **Deadhead Regularly** - Promotes continuous bloom
2. **Don't Overwater** - Tolerates dry conditions
3. **Pinch Young Plants** - Encourages bushiness
4. **Remove Spent Blooms** - Prevents self-seeding if unwanted

## Common Issues
- **Spider Mites** - In hot, dry conditions
- **Slugs** - On young plants
- **Gray Mold** - In wet conditions, improve air flow

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans
Use as: Pest deterrent, border plant, trap crop for aphids

## Garden Benefits
- May deter some pests
- Attracts beneficial insects
- Easy and reliable
- Edible flowers (remove white base)
