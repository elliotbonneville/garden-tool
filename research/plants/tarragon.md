# Tarragon

## Overview
French tarragon is a perennial herb with a distinctive anise flavor essential to French cuisine. It doesn't produce viable seeds - propagate by division.

## Varieties
- **French Tarragon** - True culinary variety, must be grown from cuttings
- **Russian Tarragon** - Inferior flavor, avoid

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 18-24 inches apart |
| Years to Maturity | 2 years |
| Water | Moderate, good drainage |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant Divisions**: Spring after last frost
- **Harvest**: Once established, ongoing
- **Divide Plants**: Every 3-4 years

## Care Tips
1. **Buy plants, not seeds** - French tarragon doesn't seed
2. **Well-drained soil** - Dislikes wet feet
3. **Divide regularly** - Keeps plants vigorous
4. **Mulch for winter** - Protect roots in Zone 6b

## Common Issues
- **Root rot** - Ensure good drainage
- **Declining vigor** - Divide plants every 3-4 years
- **Wrong variety** - Verify it's French, not Russian

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Most vegetables
Avoid planting near: Nothing specific

## Expected Yield
- Continuous light harvests throughout season
