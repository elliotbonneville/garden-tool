# Potatoes

## Overview
Potatoes are a satisfying crop that produces abundantly from a small space. Growing your own gives access to unique varieties unavailable in stores.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (6+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 5.0-6.0 (slightly acidic) |
| Spacing | 12 inches apart, rows 3 feet apart |
| Days to Harvest | 70-120 days |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: April 1 - May 15 (after last hard frost)
- **Harvest New Potatoes**: When plants flower
- **Harvest Storage Potatoes**: After vines die back

## Care Tips
1. **Hill Regularly** - Cover emerging stems with soil/mulch
2. **Don't Let Tubers See Light** - Causes greening (toxic)
3. **Stop Watering Before Harvest** - Helps cure skins
4. **Cure Before Storage** - 1-2 weeks in cool, dark place

## Common Issues
- **Colorado Potato Beetles** - Hand pick, BT for larvae
- **Late Blight** - Avoid wet foliage, destroy infected plants
- **Scab** - Keep soil acidic, don't over-lime

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beans, corn, cabbage, horseradish
Avoid planting near: Tomatoes, squash, cucumbers

## Expected Yield
5-10 lbs per plant
