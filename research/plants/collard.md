# Collard Greens

## Overview
Collards are heat-tolerant, nutritious greens that improve with frost. They're one of the easiest brassicas to grow.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Georgia Southern** - Classic heirloom
- **Vates** - Compact, cold-hardy
- **Champion** - Slow to bolt, tender

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.5-6.8 |
| Spacing | 18-24 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 60-80 days |
| Water | 1-1.5 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring**: Start indoors March, transplant April-May
- **Fall** (preferred): Direct sow July, transplant August
- **Harvest**: Ongoing, frost sweetens leaves

## Care Tips
1. **Harvest lower leaves** - Plant keeps producing
2. **Leave growing point** - Don't cut the center
3. **Overwinter possible** - Heavy mulch in Zone 6b
4. **Frost improves flavor** - Don't rush fall harvest

## Common Issues
- **Cabbage worms** - BT spray or row covers
- **Aphids** - Spray off with water
- **Yellowing leaves** - May need nitrogen

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Onions, potatoes, celery
Avoid planting near: Strawberries, tomatoes

## Expected Yield
- Continuous harvest for months from each plant
