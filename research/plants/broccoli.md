# Broccoli

## Overview
Broccoli is a nutritious cool-season crop. After harvesting the main head, plants produce smaller side shoots for extended harvest.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Calabrese** - Classic heading broccoli
- **Broccoli Raab/Rapini** - Leafy, bitter greens with small florets
- **De Cicco** - Heirloom, many side shoots

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to light shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 18-24 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 55-80 days |
| Water | 1-1.5 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring Start Indoors**: March 1-15
- **Transplant**: April 15-May 1
- **Fall Start**: July 1-15 (transplant Aug 1)
- **Harvest**: June (spring), October-November (fall)

## Care Tips
1. **Consistent moisture** - Prevents bolting
2. **Harvest before flowers open** - Tight heads best
3. **Keep harvesting side shoots** - Extends season
4. **Row covers** - Protect from cabbage worms

## Common Issues
- **Cabbage worms** - Use BT spray or row covers
- **Bolting** - Plant at right time, keep cool
- **Hollow stems** - Caused by boron deficiency

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Beets, celery, onions, potatoes
Avoid planting near: Strawberries, tomatoes

## Expected Yield
- 1 main head + many side shoots per plant
