# Scallions (Green Onions)

## Overview
Scallions are quick-growing alliums harvested for their green tops and small white bulbs. They can be harvested at any stage.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Evergreen Hardy White** - Very cold tolerant, perennial
- **Tokyo Long White** - Long white stems
- **Red Beard** - Red-tinged, mild flavor

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 1-2 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 60-80 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: February-March
- **Direct Sow**: April-August
- **Transplant**: Early spring
- **Harvest**: Ongoing once established

## Care Tips
1. **Succession plant** - Every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
2. **Cut and come again** - Harvest tops, roots regrow
3. **Overwinter** - Mulch heavily for spring harvest
4. **Thin to eat** - Use thinnings in kitchen

## Common Issues
- **Onion thrips** - Spray with water, use reflective mulch
- **Bolting** - From temperature extremes
- **Soft rot** - Avoid overwatering, good drainage

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Carrots, beets, lettuce, tomatoes
Avoid planting near: Beans, peas

## Expected Yield
- Continuous harvest, 1 bunch per square foot
