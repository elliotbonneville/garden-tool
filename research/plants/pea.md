# Peas

## Overview
Peas are a cool-season crop perfect for early spring planting. Snap peas are eaten pod and all, making them a garden favorite for fresh snacking.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Sugar Snap** - Classic snap pea, sweet and crunchy
- **Super Sugar Snap** - Improved disease resistance
- **Cascadia** - Compact, good for small spaces

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 2-4 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 60-70 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Direct Sow Spring**: March 15 - April 15
- **Fall Crop**: August 1-15
- **Harvest**: June (spring), October (fall)

## Care Tips
1. **Direct sow** - Doesn't transplant well
2. **Provide support** - Even "bush" types benefit from trellis
3. **Inoculant** - Helps nitrogen fixation
4. **Pick frequently** - Encourages more production

- **Powdery mildew** - Plant early, good air circulation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Carrots, radishes, cucumbers, beans
Avoid planting near: Onions, garlic

## Expected Yield
- 2-4 lbs per 10-foot row
