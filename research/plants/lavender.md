# Lavender

## Overview
Lavender is a fragrant perennial herb prized for its flowers and scent. Once established, it's drought-tolerant and attracts pollinators.

## Varieties for Zone 6b
- **Hidcote** - Compact, deep purple, very cold-hardy
- **Munstead** - Classic English lavender, reliable
- **Phenomenal** - Exceptionally cold and heat tolerant

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun (8+ hours) |
| Soil pH | 6.5-7.5 (slightly alkaline) |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Years to Maturity | 2-3 years |
| Water | Low - drought tolerant once established |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Plant**: Spring after frost danger
- **First Light Harvest**: Year 1
- **Full Harvest**: Year 2+
- **Bloom Time**: June-August

## Care Tips
1. **Excellent drainage** - Will not tolerate wet feet
2. **Don't overwater** - Most common killer
3. **Prune after bloom** - Cut back by 1/3, never into old wood
4. **Winter mulch** - Light mulch in Zone 6b

## Common Issues
- **Root rot** - Caused by poor drainage/overwatering
- **Winter kill** - Choose hardy varieties, ensure drainage
- **Woody growth** - Prune regularly to prevent

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Roses, other Mediterranean herbs
Avoid planting near: Shade-loving or wet-soil plants

## Expected Yield
- Multiple harvests of flower stems per season
