# Oregano

## Overview
Oregano is a hardy perennial herb essential for Mediterranean cooking. Once established, it spreads readily and requires minimal care.

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-8.0 |
| Spacing | 12-18 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 6-8 weeks from transplant |
| Water | Drought tolerant once established |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Transplant**: After last frost (May)
- **Harvest**: All season long
- **Overwinter**: Hardy perennial, cut back in fall

## Care Tips
1. **Don't Overwater** - Prefers dry conditions
2. **Harvest Before Flowering** - Best flavor
3. **Cut Back Regularly** - Promotes bushy growth
4. **Divide Every 3-4 Years** - Prevents woody center

## Common Issues
- **Leggy Growth** - Needs more sun, prune regularly
- **Root Rot** - Improve drainage, reduce watering
- **Aphids** - Spray with water, rarely serious

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Peppers, tomatoes, squash
Avoid planting near: None significant

## Expected Yield
Multiple harvests per season, dries well
