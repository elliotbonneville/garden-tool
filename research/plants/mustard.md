# Mustard Greens

## Overview
Mustard greens are spicy, fast-growing greens perfect for adding kick to salads and stir-fries. Very easy to grow in cool weather.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Southern Giant Curled** - Classic, frilly leaves
- **Red Giant** - Purple-red, milder flavor
- **Mizuna** - Japanese, mild, feathery

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 6-12 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 30-45 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring**: Direct sow March 15-April 30
- **Fall**: Direct sow August 1-September 15
- **Harvest**: Ongoing until bolt or hard frost

## Care Tips
1. **Harvest young** - Leaves are mildest when small
2. **Cut and come again** - Harvest outer leaves
3. **Bolt tolerant cooking** - Still edible after bolt
4. **Succession plant** - Every 2 weeks

## Common Issues
- **Flea beetles** - Row covers protect young plants
- **Bolting** - Plant in cool weather only
- **Hot flavor** - Harvest younger for milder taste

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Celery, onions, peas
Avoid planting near: Strawberries

## Expected Yield
- Multiple harvests, 2-4 lbs per 10-foot row
