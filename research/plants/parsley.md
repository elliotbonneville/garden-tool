# Parsley

## Overview
Parsley is a biennial herb often grown as an annual. It's highly nutritious and used in countless dishes. Slow to germinate but worth the wait.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Italian Flat-Leaf** - Stronger flavor, preferred for cooking
- **Curly** - Milder, great for garnish
- **Hamburg** - Grown for edible roots

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 6-8 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 70-90 days |
| Water | 1-2 inches per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Indoors**: March 1-15 (soak seeds first)
- **Direct Sow**: April 15-May 15
- **Transplant**: After last frost
- **Harvest**: Ongoing all season

## Care Tips
1. **Soak seeds 24hr** - Speeds up slow germination
2. **Be patient** - Takes 2-4 weeks to germinate
3. **Harvest outer leaves** - Plant keeps producing
4. **Mulch for winter** - May overwinter in Zone 6b

## Common Issues
- **Slow germination** - Normal, be patient
- **Carrot rust fly** - Use row covers
- **Bolting (year 2)** - Natural biennial behavior

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Tomatoes, asparagus, corn, roses
Avoid planting near: Lettuce, mint

## Expected Yield
- Continuous harvest all season
