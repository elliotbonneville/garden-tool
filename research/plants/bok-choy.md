# Bok Choy

## Overview
Bok choy is a fast-growing Asian green perfect for stir-fries. It thrives in cool weather and can be harvested at baby or full size.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Joi Choi** - Heat tolerant, slow to bolt
- **Shanghai** - Small, tender, quick maturing
- **Win-Win** - Baby bok choy, very fast

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun to part shade |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.5 |
| Spacing | 6-12 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 30-45 days (baby), 45-60 (full) |
| Water | Consistent moisture |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Spring**: Direct sow April 1-May 1
- **Fall** (better): Direct sow August 1-September 1
- **Harvest**: Before heat (spring) or until hard frost (fall)

## Care Tips
1. **Fall crop is easier** - Less bolting pressure
2. **Harvest outer leaves** - Or cut whole head
3. **Part shade in warmth** - Delays bolting
4. **Succession plant** - Every 2 weeks

## Common Issues
- **Bolting** - Avoid heat, plant in cool weather
- **Flea beetles** - Row covers, especially for young plants
- **Slugs** - Hand pick, use traps

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Other brassicas, beets, onions
Avoid planting near: Strawberries

## Expected Yield
- 1 head or multiple harvests per plant
