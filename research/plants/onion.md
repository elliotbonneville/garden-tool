# Onions

## Overview
Onions are a kitchen staple that store well. Choose the right day-length variety for your latitude for best bulb formation.

## Varieties for This Garden
- **Long-day varieties** - Best for Zone 6b (north of 35Â° latitude)

## Growing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Sun | Full sun |
| Soil pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Spacing | 4-6 inches apart |
| Days to Harvest | 90-120 days |
| Water | 1 inch per week |

## Planting Timeline (Zone 6b)
- **Start Seeds Indoors**: January - February
- **Transplant/Sets**: March 15 - April 15
- **Harvest**: When tops fall over (July-August)

## Care Tips
1. **Day Length Matters** - Long-day for northern gardens
2. **Keep Weeded** - Onions don't compete well
3. **Stop Watering** - When tops start falling
4. **Cure Well** - 2-3 weeks in dry, airy spot

## Common Issues
- **No Bulb Formation** - Wrong day-length variety
- **Bolting** - Temperature stress, plant at right time
- **Onion Maggots** - Row cover, crop rotation

## Companion Plants
Good companions: Carrots, lettuce, tomatoes, cabbage
Avoid planting near: Beans, peas

## Expected Yield
1 onion per plant
